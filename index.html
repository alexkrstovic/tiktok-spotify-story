What goes viral often gets streamed-but not always

Big Picture

TikTok and Spotify are connected - but it's complicated

Four thousand songs, four thousand stories. Each dot represents a track: the further right it sits, the more it blew up on TikTok. The higher it sits, the more it was streamed on Spotify.

If TikTok buzz always led to Spotify success, these dots would form a straight, predictable line. Instead, there is only a vast, chaotic cloud.

The data shows two different worlds. On one side are the streaming giants who don't need a viral video to stay on top. On the other are viral sensations that dominated TikTok, only to be ignored on Spotify. TikTok can spark interest, but it cannot force a hit.

Discovering the Trends

A clear pattern - with one strange twist

Group all 4,000 songs by how much TikTok activity they had, then measure their average Spotify streams. A pattern emerges: more TikTok presence means more streams.

The exception is the Viral tier. Songs with over 10 million TikTok posts average fewer streams than songs in the tier below them. Maximum exposure does not equal maximum success.

Artists Behind the Hits

Streaming giants aren't always TikTok giants

The biggest names in streaming often have a TikTok presence that barely registers by comparison. They built their audiences before short-form video changed the game.

Surprises & Outliers

The Unexpected Results

Two kinds of songs break the pattern completely. Some became the soundtrack to billions of TikTok videos - and still barely registered on Spotify. Others amassed billions of streams without a single TikTok post to their name.

Viral but understreamed

Take "Monkeys Spinning Monkeys" by Kevin MacLeod. It has been used in 233 billion TikTok videos. You have almost certainly heard it. It has 11 million Spotify streams - a fraction of what that exposure should produce. The sound lived on TikTok. It never made the jump.

Huge on Spotify, invisible on TikTok

"Bohemian Rhapsody" by Queen has 2.5 billion Spotify streams and zero TikTok posts. Not one. It was built on radio, albums, and word of mouth across decades - long before short-form video existed. TikTok had nothing to do with it.

The Verdict

TikTok is a tool. Not a guarantee.

TikTok helps - but only up to a point. Songs with serious TikTok activity average significantly more streams than songs with none. Go further into full virality and the numbers actually drop. The moment burns out before it converts.

The songs and artists that dominate Spotify built their audiences through something TikTok cannot manufacture. A fanbase that keeps coming back long after the trend has moved on.
